## Design Context

### Users
Small business owners — operators wearing many hats. They're non-technical, time-poor, and anxious about cash flow. They open FlowDesk when they need to know if they can make payroll, chase an invoice, or understand why last month was down. They want answers, not dashboards to configure.

### Brand Personality
**Sharp. Empowering. Clear.**

FlowDesk is the co-pilot for small business owners who don't have a CFO. The tone is confident without being arrogant — like a sharp advisor who speaks plainly, gives you the answer, and tells you what to do next. Not calm and measured (too passive), not playful (too lightweight for financial decisions). Sharp, direct, trustworthy.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark mode — deep navy/indigo palette (#080614 base, #4F46FF accent)
- **Feel**: Premium and polished, not generic SaaS. Should feel like a flagship product, not an MVP.
- **Anti-references**: Not Notion-bland, not Bloomberg-stuffy, not another purple-gradient AI product. Avoid generic glassmorphism overuse.
- **Typography**: Syne (display) + DM Sans (body) + DM Mono (numbers) — already in place, lean into it
- **Fonts to avoid**: Inter, Roboto, system defaults

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity first** — Every element must earn its place. If it doesn't help a small business owner make a decision, cut it.
2. **Sharp, not soft** — Bold typography, confident copy, decisive visual hierarchy. No timid spacing or wishy-washy gradients.
3. **Premium by default** — Every interaction state, hover, and transition must feel intentional. No missing states.
4. **Speed signals trust** — Animations should be fast and purposeful (150–250ms). Slow animations undermine confidence.
5. **Plain English** — Copy should match the product's promise: no jargon, no fluff, no passive voice.
